## (B) Key Forms and Filing Sequence

The channel covers in detail the work with three key forms:
- **I-131 (Re-parole)** — online filing via USCIS online portal
- **I-765 (Work Permit/EAD)** — filing for parole extended persons  
- **I-912 (Fee Waiver)** — for fee exemption
- **G-1145 (Electronic Submission Notification)** — for online submission confirmation
- **G-1450/G-1650 (Payment Card)** — for online payment in certain scenarios

### Filing Sequence Options:

#### Online Filing I-131
- **Cost:** $580 (re-parole only) or $1,325 (re-parole + parole extension)
- **Process:** Standard completion via online USCIS portal with fee payment via credit/debit card

#### PDF Online 3 I-912
- **Process:** Submitting filled PDF-form I-131 and I-912 in PDF format through USCIS online portal for fee-exempt (free) filing
- **Advantage:** No fee, but requires understanding PDF form fields

#### Postal Filing
- **Method:** Physical packet of I-131 + I-912 + HR-1 submitted by mail
- **Timing:** Subject to postal delays; envelope tracking via certified mail recommended

#### Scenario: EAD Notice Without Active Parole
- If applicant filed form I-131 but has not yet received active parole, they receive Receipt Notice only per I-765
- Applicant must wait until valid I-131 approval before proceeding with I-765
- Basic principle: I-765 permit requires valid active parole foundation (I-131 approved status)

#### Scenario: Re-Parole RFE (Request for Evidence)
- When filing I-131 online, there is a 500-character limit for the explanation/RFE reason field
- Essential to briefly but comprehensively explain the re-parole reason in this field
- Documentation (Cover letter and evidence) must accompany in the supporting documents section
- RFE response requires careful description of circumstances and supporting documents/evidence in proper supporting documents folder

## (C) Fee Waiver Strategy (I-912 + HR-1)

To obtain exemption from fee payment, form I-912 (Request for Fee Waiver) is used with the following conditions and evidence requirements.

### Grounds for Filing I-912
- Most common grounds: receipt of government benefits (SNAP, Medicaid, Food Stamps, WIC, housing assistance)
- Point to **Line 1.a of form I-912** — eligibility based on receiving government assistance

### Evidence Requirements for Fee Waiver
- **Medical insurance scan:** Both sides required
- **Official benefits verification letter:** From Welfare/Benefits department indicating:
  - Dates of benefits received
  - Persons on whose benefit the applicant is relying
  - Data received and specific types of benefits
  - Signature of welfare official confirming accuracy

### Critical Mistake: Family I-912 Error (Corrected Rule)
- **Previous rule (outdated):** Only one I-912 could be filed for entire family
- **Current rule:** Form I-912 is now filed separately; only the individual with primary benefit eligibility (Requestor = person with direct benefit entitlement) has the right to file

### Documentation Strategy
- Maintain clear evidence of direct benefit receipt
- If family members have benefits, each eligible member files separate I-912
- Ensure official verification letter explicitly names the applicant as benefits recipient

## (D) Mistakes and Prevention

### Mistake 1: Name Change Pronunciation Issues (Transliteration)
When filing, must indicate ALL previous names (including maiden names, etc.) to avoid discrepancies:
- Include name variations due to transliteration (Cyrillic to Latin conversion)
- Specify which names are maiden names, previous married names, etc.
- Helps system match records across different name formats

### Mistake 2: Missing Supporting Evidence
- Filing I-131 without supporting documentation/evidence guarantees rejection
- Rejection typically leads to RFE (Request for Evidence)
- Applicant must demonstrate parole-eligible grounds with supporting evidence

### Mistake 3: Incomplete Document Preparation
- Scans must be clear, high-quality
- Both sides required for ID documents (passport, driver's license, medical insurance)
- Cover letters must clearly explain circumstances and references to enclosed evidence

## (E) Documents for Attachment

For successful filing, the following actual scans must be prepared:

### Required Documents

1. **Закордонний паспорт (Foreign Passport)**
   - First page AND last page with passport number
   - If passport is expired, include both pages of expired passport + first page of new passport

2. **Форма I-94**
   - Obtained from CBP i94.cbp.dhs.gov

3. **Картка дозволу на працю (EAD — Work Permit Card)**
   - BOTH SIDES required

4. **Водійське посвідчення або State ID (Driver's License or State ID)**
   - BOTH SIDES required

5. **Супровідний лист (Cover Letter)**
   - Explanation of circumstances and supporting evidence
   - Clear reference to what evidence is enclosed

6. **Для дітей додатково (For Children — Additional)**
   - Birth certificate with translation
   - Translation certified in another language (translation must include certified translation statement about translation accuracy)
   - Scan of first page of parent's passport
