## Who may use this form

Per Instructions PDF (01/20/25):

- Lawful permanent residents or conditional permanent residents applying for a **Reentry Permit**
- Aliens in valid refugee or asylee status applying for a **Refugee Travel Document**
- LPRs as a direct result of refugee/asylee status applying for a **Refugee Travel Document**
- TPS beneficiaries inside the U.S. applying for a **TPS Travel Authorization Document** (INA 244(f)(3))
- Aliens inside the U.S. applying for an **Advance Parole Document** (various bases: pending I-485, I-589, I-821, DACA, TPS, parolee under INA 212(d)(5), CNMI long-term resident, etc.)
- Aliens outside the U.S. applying for an **Initial Parole Document** under specific programs (FWVP, IMMVI, intergovernmental referral, FRTF) or on general humanitarian/significant public benefit grounds
- Aliens inside the U.S. applying for **Parole in Place** (Military PIP, FRTF, other)
- Previously paroled aliens inside the U.S. requesting **Re-parole** — including Ukrainian citizens paroled on or after Feb 11, 2022 (select Part 1 Item 10.C)

## Warnings

### Critical

- **Wrong box for Ukrainian re-parole**: Must select Part 1 Item 10.C. Other boxes (e.g., outside-U.S. travel document types) are wrong and may cause rejection. — Source: Form I-131 Instructions 01/20/25; community forensic audit research
- **Personal evidence required since August 2025**: Form-only filing for re-parole is no longer sufficient. USCIS requires personal evidence (medical conditions, family ties, employment, etc.). General "war in Ukraine" justification rejected. — Source: USCIS humanitarian parole guidance

### Caution

- **Biometrics required**: Most applicants aged 14–79 must appear at a USCIS Application Support Center (ASC). USCIS sends appointment notice after form receipt. — Source: Instructions 01/20/25, Section 9
- **DHS may revoke parole at any time**, including while the alien is in the U.S. — Source: Instructions 01/20/25, Section 8
- **Additional $280 non-waivable fee** when requesting EAD in Part 9 with re-parole. Must be paid separately. — Source: G-1055 Appendix B
- **180-day filing window for re-parole**: Confusion about when to file. USCIS recommends filing 150–180 days before expiration.

### Info

- Filing I-131 for re-parole does NOT automatically grant work authorization. A separate I-765 or Part 9 EAD request is needed.
- For re-parole applicants, USCIS will not issue EAD until the new parole period is approved.

## Common mistakes (from community research)

Per `data/common-mistakes-by-form.md` (forensic audit research, pre-validated):

1. Checking the wrong box — Ukrainian re-parole filers should select Box 10.C, not "outside US" travel document boxes
2. Filing too early — confusion about the 180-day window for re-parole submissions
3. Form-only filing in 2026 — since August 2025, USCIS requires PERSONAL evidence (medical, family, employment), not just general "war in Ukraine" justification
4. Incorrect parole expiration date entry — copying from wrong document
5. Not including the original Travel Authorization document with the application
6. Missing biometrics fee for first-time filers (the $85 fee is separate from the filing fee in some categories)
