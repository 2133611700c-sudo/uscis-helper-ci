## 1. Increasing the Fee for Certain Aliens Ordered Removed in Absentia (HR-1 Implementation)

**Source:** DHS / Federal Register — https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/05/20/2026-10082/increasing-the-fee-for-certain-aliens-ordered-removed-in-absentia-as-established-by-the-hr-1
**Published:** May 20, 2026
**Effective date:** Not specified (proposed rule)
**Document #:** 2026-10082

### What changed
DHS is proposing to increase the fee for certain aliens ordered removed in absentia who fail to depart the United States and are subsequently arrested by ICE. The proposed fee increase is from $5,130 to $18,000. The rule also establishes that DHS will adjust this fee for inflation annually. This is part of the implementation of Section 100016 of the Budget Reconciliation Act (HR-1 / "One Big Beautiful Bill Act").

### What was known before
The April 28, 2026 USCIS Alert ("DHS Announces Consequences for Unpaid Annual Asylum Fees, Unveils New H.R. 1 Requirements") signaled broader HR-1 implementation affecting immigration fees. The original fee of $5,130 was established by the HR-1 reconciliation bill. This proposed rule seeks to more than triple that amount.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- FAQ section — any content referencing immigration fees or consequences of missed hearings
- User-facing guidance about attending all USCIS/EOIR appointments and the consequences of in absentia orders
- Any general fee-related informational pages

### Recommended action
- No immediate product change needed — this is a proposed rule, not yet final
- Monitor for the final rule publication, which will set the effective date
- Consider adding a note in user-facing content about the importance of attending all immigration court hearings, given the escalating financial penalties for in absentia removal orders

### Risk level
**LOW**
- This is a proposed rule (not final) and applies specifically to individuals ordered removed in absentia, not to application or EAD fees. However, it signals active HR-1 fee implementation that may affect other fee categories in the near future.

## 2. Employment Eligibility Verification — Information Collection Extension (No Change)

**Source:** DHS / USCIS / Federal Register — https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/05/18/2026-09846/agency-information-collection-activities-extension-without-change-of-a-currently-approved-collection
**Published:** May 18, 2026
**Effective date:** Not specified
**Document #:** 2026-09846

### What changed
USCIS published a Paperwork Reduction Act notice requesting public comment on the extension (without change) of the currently approved Employment Eligibility Verification information collection (Form I-9 process). This is a routine administrative renewal — no substantive changes to the employment eligibility verification process are proposed.

### What was known before
The I-9 employment eligibility verification process has been in place. A companion notice (doc 2026-09845, same date) extends the E-Verify+ collection, also without change.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- No direct Messenginfo service impact identified
- The I-9 process is employer-side; however, Messenginfo users who receive EADs use them as List A documents for I-9 verification

### Recommended action
- No action needed — routine extension with no changes

### Risk level
**LOW**
- Informational only. No changes to the employment verification process.
