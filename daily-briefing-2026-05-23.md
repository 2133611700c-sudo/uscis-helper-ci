## 1. HR.1 Fee Rule — Effective May 29, 2026 (6 DAYS AWAY)
**Source:** Federal Register, USCIS — https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/29/2026-08333/uscis-immigration-fees-and-related-procedures-required-by-hr1-reconciliation-bill
**Published:** 2026-04-29
**Effective date:** 2026-05-29
**Document #:** 2026-08333

### What changed
DHS issued an interim final rule codifying immigration fees required by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (H.R.1). This includes asylum filing fees, new Form I-94 fee requirements, and retention of Form I-589 filing fee. The I-765 Initial Parole EAD fee is $560 (up from $550), and the I-765 Renewal/Extension Parole EAD fee is $280 (up from $275). Comment period runs through June 29, 2026.

### What was known before
FY2026 inflation adjustments to HR-1 fees were announced in November 2025 (effective January 1, 2026). This new IFR codifies additional fee procedures beyond the inflation adjustments.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- Pricing page — I-765 fee amounts need updating if not already reflecting $560/$280
- TPS section — any references to EAD filing fees
- FAQ — fee waiver (I-912) guidance may need review against new fee structure
- Translation service instructions — any forms mentioning filing fees

### Recommended action
- **IMMEDIATE**: Verify all fee references across the site match the new amounts effective May 29
- Update the fee calculator/guidance if one exists
- Add a banner or notice about the May 29 effective date

### Risk level
**HIGH** — Fee change effective in 6 days. Any user filing after May 29 with old fee amounts will have their application rejected.

## 2. Signatures on Immigration Benefit Requests — New Rule
**Source:** Federal Register, DHS — https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/05/11/2026-09289/signatures-on-immigration-benefit-requests
**Published:** 2026-05-11
**Effective date:** 2026-07-10
**Document #:** 2026-09289

### What changed
DHS is amending 8 CFR 103.2(a)(7) to allow USCIS adjudicators to reject or deny benefit requests with invalid signatures discovered after acceptance. Previously, once a form was accepted, an invalid signature could not serve as grounds for denial. Under the new rule, USCIS may retain the filing fee and consider the application fully adjudicated and the applicant ineligible. Applies to all requests submitted on or after July 10, 2026.

### What was known before
Prior regulations did not explicitly allow USCIS to deny a benefit request solely on the basis of an invalid signature found after acceptance.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- Form-filling service (I-765, I-821, I-131) — signature guidance must be prominent
- User instructions — must emphasize correct signature requirements
- FAQ — add guidance about what constitutes a valid signature

### Recommended action
- Add clear signature instructions to all form preparation workflows
- Update user-facing copy to warn that invalid signatures can now result in denial AND fee forfeiture
- No immediate action needed — effective July 10, but preparation should start now

### Risk level
**MEDIUM** — Effective July 10. Users preparing forms through Messenginfo need clear guidance to avoid costly signature errors.

## 3. Administrative Hold on U4U Benefit Requests
**Source:** USCIS Electronic Reading Room — https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/foia/AdministrativeHoldonAllUSCISBenefitRequestsFiledbyParoleesUnderU4U_CHNVorFRP.pdf
**Published:** not specified (discovered via FOIA reading room)
**Effective date:** not specified
**Document #:** not specified

### What changed
USCIS issued a memorandum authorizing an immediate USCIS-wide administrative hold on all pending benefit requests filed by aliens who are or were paroled into the United States under the Uniting for Ukraine (U4U), CHNV, or Family Reunification Parole (FRP) processes. This means all pending applications from U4U parolees — including I-765 EAD applications and I-821 TPS applications — may be subject to this hold.

### What was known before
The January 20, 2025 Executive Order "Securing Our Borders" paused acceptance of Form I-134A (supporter declarations). This administrative hold extends the freeze to all benefit requests from U4U/CHNV/FRP parolees.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- U4U/re-parole guidance pages — must reflect the hold status
- EAD application guidance for U4U parolees — users need to know their applications may be on hold
- FAQ — critical update needed for U4U users asking about application status
- Translation service — users may still need translations for pending applications but should understand processing is paused

### Recommended action
- **IMMEDIATE**: Add prominent notice on all U4U-related pages about the administrative hold
- Update FAQ with guidance for U4U parolees with pending benefit requests
- Do NOT advise users to stop filing — the hold is on adjudication, not acceptance

### Risk level
**HIGH** — Directly affects the primary user base (Ukrainian parolees). Users need to understand their applications are on hold.

## 4. I-134A Acceptance Paused — U4U Re-Parole Blocked
**Source:** USCIS — https://www.uscis.gov/humanitarian/uniting-for-ukraine/frequently-asked-questions-about-uniting-for-ukraine
**Published:** post-January 20, 2025 (ongoing)
**Effective date:** January 20, 2025 (ongoing)
**Document #:** not applicable

### What changed
USCIS continues to pause acceptance of Form I-134A, Online Request to be a Supporter and Declaration of Financial Support, pending review of all categorical parole processes per the January 20, 2025 Executive Order. This effectively blocks new U4U parole applications and re-parole requests that require I-134A.

### What was known before
This pause has been in effect since January 2025. No change in status, but this remains the operative reality for Ukrainian parolees whose parole periods are expiring.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- Re-parole guidance pages — must clearly state I-134A is not being accepted
- U4U landing page — status update needed
- User-facing copy — should not suggest re-parole is available

### Recommended action
- Verify all U4U/re-parole pages clearly state I-134A acceptance remains paused
- No new action if already updated — monitor for any change in status

### Risk level
**MEDIUM** — Ongoing situation. Users whose parole is expiring need to understand alternative pathways (e.g., TPS if eligible).
