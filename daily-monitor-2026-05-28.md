## 1. USCIS Will Grant Adjustment of Status Only in Extraordinary Circumstances
**Source:** USCIS Newsroom — https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/news-releases/us-citizenship-and-immigration-services-will-grant-adjustment-of-status-only-in-extraordinary
**Published:** 2026-05-22
**Effective date:** 2026-05-22 (policy memo effective immediately)
**Document #:** PM-602-0199

### What changed
USCIS issued a policy memo directing that adjustment of status (I-485) will only be granted in extraordinary circumstances. Applicants seeking green cards are now expected to process through consular immigrant visa processing at US embassies/consulates abroad, rather than adjusting status domestically. Officers must evaluate each case individually and exercise heightened discretion.

### What was known before
Adjustment of status under INA §245 has historically been a commonly used pathway for eligible applicants already present in the US (e.g., TPS holders, parolees with approved immigrant petitions) to obtain lawful permanent residence without departing. This change significantly narrows domestic AOS availability.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- FAQ section on "Path to Green Card" for Ukrainian TPS holders and U4U parolees
- Any user-facing guidance suggesting adjustment of status as a routine option
- Translation service — I-485 instructions and related content may need contextual updates
- Potential impact on users who were planning to file I-485 domestically

### Recommended action
- **HIGH PRIORITY:** Add alert banner to any page discussing adjustment of status
- Update FAQ to reflect that AOS is now discretionary/extraordinary only
- Add informational note (NOT legal advice) that consular processing is now the expected pathway
- Monitor for litigation challenging this policy memo

### Risk level
**HIGH** — Directly affects user workflow and expectations for Ukrainian immigrants who may have been planning domestic AOS. Update immediately.

## 2. Signatures on Immigration Benefit Requests — Final Rule
**Source:** Federal Register — https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/05/11/2026-09289/signatures-on-immigration-benefit-requests
**Published:** 2026-05-11
**Effective date:** 2026-07-10
**Document #:** 2026-09289

### What changed
USCIS published a final rule clarifying provisions governing submission of benefit requests to reduce risks from invalid signatures. The rule amends regulations on how applicants, petitioners, and representatives must sign immigration forms. Forms submitted on or after July 10, 2026 must comply with the new signature requirements.

### What was known before
Prior rules had less stringent or less specific requirements around valid signatures on immigration benefit requests. This tightening aligns with broader DHS fraud-prevention efforts.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- All form-filling services (I-765, I-821, I-131, I-912, etc.) — signature guidance sections

- Update form-filling instructions for all supported forms to reflect new signature requirements
- Add note about new rules in form preparation workflow
- Timeline: update within 2 weeks (before July 10 effective date)

### Risk level
**MEDIUM** — Affects form submission procedures for all users. Must update before July 10, 2026 effective date.

## 4. Deferred Action Policy Alert — Extraordinary Use of Prosecutorial Discretion
**Source:** USCIS Policy Manual Update — https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/policy-manual-updates/20260508-DeferredAction.pdf
**Published:** 2026-05-08
**Effective date:** 2026-05-08 (immediate); SIJ change effective 2026-05-10
**Document #:** Policy Alert (not Federal Register)

### What changed
USCIS reaffirmed that deferred action is an extraordinary use of prosecutorial discretion, not a routine benefit. Common hardships that any removable alien may experience are insufficient to warrant deferred action. Additionally, for SIJ-based Forms I-360 filed on or after May 10, 2026, USCIS will no longer automatically conduct deferred action determinations for Special Immigrant Juveniles who cannot adjust status because a visa is unavailable.

### What was known before
Deferred action had been used more broadly in prior administrations. The current administration has been tightening discretionary relief across the board.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- FAQ on deferred action (if any content exists)
- Any user guidance mentioning deferred action as an option for Ukrainians facing removal
- General immigration options overview pages

### Recommended action
- Review any platform content referencing deferred action
- If deferred action is mentioned as an option for users, add context that it is now reserved for extraordinary circumstances only
- No urgent action needed unless deferred action is prominently featured in user-facing content

### Risk level
**MEDIUM** — Affects users who may be considering deferred action requests. Update within 1 week.
